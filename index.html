Problem Statement

Arabic, one of the world's most widely spoken languages, exhibits a rich diversity of dialects across different regions in the Middle East. These dialects often pose challenges in natural language processing tasks such as speech recognition, sentiment analysis, and machine translation. Understanding and classifying these dialects is crucial for developing language technologies that can cater to the linguistic nuances of various Arabic-speaking communities. Therefore, the problem statement for this project is to build a deep-learning model for Arabic dialects classification.

Dataset

Our chosen dataset, the Arabic Online Commentary Dataset, is a remarkable collection of over 52 million words. It stands out as an essential tool for research, especially in the study of dialects. The dataset's uniqueness lies in the extensive and meticulous annotation process. Each sentence undergoes detailed examination to classify both the dialect level and the exact dialect it represents. As of now, it has accurately annotated 108,000 sentences and a significant portion, about 41%, of these sentences display unique dialectal features.

Proposed Updates

Update #1: Different Embedding Models

We used different embedding models, and initial results have increased specially when using the CBOW (Continuous Bag of Words) embedding pre-trained model.

Update #2: Data Augmentation

We used different data augmentation techniques to balance the class distribution in our dataset. The augmentation process aimed to enhance the model's ability to generalize across different dialects.

The augmentation techniques used include:

  • N-grams
  • Random deletion
  • Random swap
Update #3: Attention Techniques

We used different attention techniques to enhance the model's ability to capture the most important features in the input sentence. The attention techniques used are: Self Attention and Bahdanau Attention.

The attention techniques used are:

  • Self Attention
  • Bahdanau Attention
Update #4: Tuning and different regularizations

We worked on the hyperparameters tuning and used different regularizers to enhance the model's accuracy.

The regularizers used are:

  • Dropout
  • L1
  • L2
Update #5: Data Preprocessing

We used different data preprocessing techniques to enhance the model's accuracy, like Camel Normalization.

Comparison with Original Model and State of the Art

Through strategic data augmentation we have enhanced the quality of our dataset, leading to a notable performance boost in our models. Our refined approach along with hyperparameters tuning resulted in a commendable accuracy of 82.40% with the CNN architecture in model B, surpassing the state-of-the-art results, which peaked at 75.21%.
